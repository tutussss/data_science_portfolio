How to do it…

  1. Right-click on the Year field under Dimensions.
  1. In the drop-down menu, navigate to Create | Group…:

  1. In the Field Name field, change the name of the group to Decade.
  2. In the Create Group [Year] window, select years 1990 through 1999 by clicking on the first and the last year while holding the Shift button on the keyboard.
  3. When the years are selected, click on Group at the bottom of the list or, alternatively, right-click on the selection and select Group:

  1. A new group will appear, called 1990, 1991, 1992 and 7 more. The name of the group will be enabled for editing by default. Rename the group to 1990s as shown in the following screenshot:

  1. Now, select years 2000 through 2009 and click the Group button.
  2. Rename the group to 2000s.
  3. Finally, select the remaining years—2010 through 2019 and click on the Group button to group them.
  4. Rename the group to 2010s.
  5. When all three groups are created and renamed, click OK.
  6. The new group has now appeared under Dimensions in the Data pane, denoted by a paperclip symbol:. Drag and drop Decade into the Rows shelf.
  7. Drag and drop Employment from Measures onto Text in the Marks card.
  8. Hover over the AVG(Employment) pill in the Marks card and click on the white arrow that appears on it.
