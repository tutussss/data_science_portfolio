Switching between dimensions with parameters

  1. Connect to the data sources.
  2. Click on the Sheet 1 tab in the bottom of the workbook.
  3. In the Data pane, make sure the Internet_usage data source is active.
  4. Click on the black downward-pointing arrow to the right of Dimensions and select Create Parameter...:

  1. In the Create Parameter window, change the name of the parameter from Parameter 1 to Region or Settlement Type.
  2. In the Data type drop-down menu, choose String.
  3. Under Allowable values, change the selection from the default of All to List.
  4. In the List of values pane that appears, click on the Click to add new value placeholder and type Region.
  1. In the row under add another value, Settlement Type, as shown in the following screenshot:

  1. Click on OK.
  2. A new section, Parameters, will appear under Measures, showing the parameter you just made (Region or Settlement Type). Hover over the Region or Settlement Type pill until a white arrow appears, click on it, and choose Show Parameter Control:

  1. Letâ€™s create a calculated field that we will use in our visualization to switch between dimensions. Click on the black downward-pointing arrow to the right of Dimensions and select Create Calculated Field...:

  1. When the calculated field window opens, change the name from Calculation1 to Switching, click on Apply, and type the following expression:
CASE [Region or Settlement Type] 
when "Region" then [Area] 
when "Settlement Type" then [Settlement type] 
END

  4. Drag and drop Internet penetration from Measures into the Rows shelf.
  5. Hover over the Internet penetration pill so that a small downward arrow appears and click on it.
  6. Navigate to Measure (Sum), and in the drop-down menu, select Average:
